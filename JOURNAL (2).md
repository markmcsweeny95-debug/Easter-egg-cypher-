I had to use a friction mount because the plastic wall was no longer thick enough to support using a screw to hold in the joystick. It has some small chamfers at the top which allow the joystick to enter smoothly.

The gap all the way through the lid for the OLED is for the wires to go through so that they are not visible from the front.

The rounded surface should also make it less likely to cause the children harm than a sharp edge and adds to the easter aesthetic a bit more. 
- -The drafted lip was designed so that the lid can have a push fit connection with the base of the model meaning no glue has to be used and it can be taken back off for repairing inside elctronics. Brainstorming (live in the devlog)...
-     Context 
- Every year around easter time i host an easter egg hunt for a family friends children who are 4 and 9 this year. The easter egg hunt i plan to set out will be where they get a riddle clue which will lead them to the next egg to find. Each of the eggs will have a clue on it but this year i plan to put on a selection of easter related symbols instead and the job of the decypher is so they can input the selection of symbols they find on the back of each paper egg into it using the joystick by flicking it in 1 of 4 directions for each symbol. Once they do this the product will output to the children the next clue of what they are looking to find and this will continue until they find the last paper egg which will have a clue to the final chocolate egg location. 

-     What is it? 
- The product is going to be a  portable handheld device which will be programmed in such a way that every input of the joystick is unique. The joystick will have 4 positions in which it can be positioned and each of these will correlate to an easter icon (egg, 
chick, bunny, flower). 

-     How does it work?
- When one postition is selected it will wait a set period of time to check if the user will input a second position and it will then wait again (this process repeats until the user doesn't touch the joystick for a few secs and the code assumes they have finished entering the position of sequences) once it has confirmed they have finished their sequence the code will search for this sequence in the datebase and output the word that was stored in the database for that sequence onto the small OLED screen built in. The clue will stay on screen until the next joystick position is sensed (until they find the next clue and start decoding it)

-     Who is it for?
- My project is designed to be used by small children which are family friends and will be designed for children between the age of 3 and 9. Because of the youngest child being 3, almost 4 i should design my product so that it can either fit into their hands or it should be held by the older child and placed on the ground to use. I should also consider the simplicity of the product to use since it may be confusing to understand for the 4 year old. My plan to combat this is by eliminating the need for words for most of it (apart from outputting them on the screen) in this case the older child will have to read out the words, although the younger child will be able to find the clues and enter them into the decypher still with no limitations. The younger child will also get to help in solving the riddles that will be shown on the screen. 

-     The design 
The physical design of the product will be 3d printed and possibly painted to look more eastery looking. The design will be 3d modelled in onshape. The design will need to be big enough to fit all the parts and as well as have holes in the right places for it to be ergenomic- ish to use.
